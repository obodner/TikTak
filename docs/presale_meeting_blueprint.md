# B2B Presale Meeting Blueprint: Community Committees (Vaads)

This guide outlines the structure, positioning, and discovery flow for targeted, 30-minute sales meetings with community committees (Vaad) in Israel.

---

## 1. Meeting Overview & Rhythm

A B2B presale meeting with a community committee should not feel like a presentation. It is a structured conversation aimed at exposing friction in their current workflow and positioning TikTak as a solution that increases transparency, saves time, and organizes chaos.

### 30-Minute Meeting Timeline

| Time | Phase | Focus / Activity | Objective |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **00:00 - 00:02** | Rapport & Agenda (2m) | Set expectations & respect their time. | Establish trust, lower sales defenses. |
| **00:02 - 00:17** | Guided Discovery (15m) | Ask strategic questions about their process. | Identify workflow gaps and pain points. |
| **00:17 - 00:27** | Solution Reveal (10m) | Show TikTak (2m demo & pilot value). | Tie TikTak directly to their uncovered pains. |
| **00:27 - 00:30** | Next Steps (3m) | Propose free 30-day transparency trial. | Secure a concrete commit date for a pilot. |

---

## 2. Phase-by-Phase Execution

### Phase 1: Rapport & The "Permission" Agenda (2 Minutes)
Acknowledge that they are volunteers and busy professionals. Frame the meeting around feedback and value rather than a hard pitch.

* **Script Outline**:
  > *"Thank you for taking the time. I know managing a community committee is a heavy lift, especially with multiple stakeholders, so I'll keep this strictly to 30 minutes. My goal today is to share what we're building to solve community maintenance headaches, but more importantly, understand if this actually solves your day-to-day coordination challenges. If it does, we can discuss how to pilot it. If not, your feedback is incredibly valuable. Does that sound fair?"*

---

### Phase 2: Guided Discovery (15 Minutes)
Ask questions that guide the Vaad to realize the limitations of their current tools (e.g., WhatsApp, spreadsheets, or physical logbooks). Focus on transparency, accountability, and the division of labor between the **volunteers (Vaad members)**, the **General Manager (Mankal)**, and the **Secretary**.

#### Guided Questions:
1. **The Resident's Entry Point**:
   > *"When a community resident spots a maintenance hazard or broken facility today, how does that report actually get to your team?"*
2. **Stakeholder Collaboration & Tracking (The "Multi-Owner" Trap)**:
   > *"In Israel, a community Vaad typically has 5-7 volunteers, a General Manager (Mankal), and a secretary. As a Vaad, how do you currently follow up on incidents reported to ensure nothing falls between the cracks?"*
3. **Resolution Times & Accountability**:
   > *"How do you keep track of how much time it takes to solve tickets once they are reported?"*
4. **Transparency & Friction**:
   > *"Once a ticket is resolved—or if it is delayed—how do you close the loop with the resident who reported it? Does this process create extra follow-ups or friction on your personal phones/WhatsApp?"*

---

### Phase 3: The "Snap & Send" Reveal (10 Minutes)
Introduce TikTak as the direct answer to the concerns raised during Discovery. Emphasize that TikTak is built for **communities**, offering zero resident onboarding, clear dashboards for the management team, and instant WhatsApp integration.

* **Key Pillars to Highlight**:
  * **Zero-Friction Resident Input**: Residents scan a location-specific QR code, snap a picture, and send. No apps, no accounts, no Hebrew-to-English translation barriers.
  * **Centralized Dashboard for the Vaad**: The Mankal, secretary, and volunteers share a single, clear dashboard view. Everyone sees the status (New, In Progress, Resolved) and who is handling it.
  * **Automated Accountability**: The dashboard automatically tracks open times, resolution durations, and logs which contractor was contacted via the WhatsApp Link Engine.

* **Live Demo (2 mins)**:
  Have them scan a sample QR code on your screen or phone to experience the "Snap & Send" reporter flow firsthand. Simultaneously show how the ticket immediately populates on the admin dashboard.

---

### Phase 4: Next Steps & The Commitment (3 Minutes)
Instead of asking for a purchase, offer a low-friction pilot focused on visibility and management efficiency.

* **Script Outline**:
  > *"We are onboarding a select group of community committees next month for a trial. We will set up your community's QR signs and your admin dashboard for free. If you find that this increases visibility, makes managing tickets clearer, and saves your Mankal and secretary valuable time, we can discuss a long-term subscription. If not, you can simply take the signs down. Would you be open to triaging this in your community starting [Proposed Date]?"*
